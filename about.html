    About Me

    Hello! I’m Aaliyah, a 27-year-old Egyptian living in China. My native language is Arabic, and I’m fluent in English and Chinese.

    My journey with Chinese began in 2014 when I first heard the language. Unlike many, I instantly fell in love with how it sounded. That fascination stayed with me through my final school years until, in 2016, I decided to pursue a bachelor’s degree in Chinese Language and Literature.

    As a writer, I was often frustrated by my inability to fully express my thoughts in English. I’ve always felt that English, as a language, sometimes fails to capture the depth of emotion and thought I want to convey. So, I searched for a language with more layers, and what better choice than what’s often considered the mother of Asian languages?

    In 2017, I joined a summer camp organized by Cairo University’s Confucius Institute and attended classes at Peking University. While others were bidding farewell to Beijing on the last day, I knew my story with this country had only just begun. A year later, I spent a year at Zhejiang University on a Confucius Institute scholarship. After graduating top of my class from Cairo University, I was awarded the Chinese Government Scholarship and returned to China in 2023 to pursue a master’s degree in Communication at Nanjing University.

    Living in China has taught me far more than any textbook ever could. My curiosity to experience “real China” led me to spend Chinese New Year with a family in Chongqing and to attend a wedding in a small village — two of my most cherished experiences that allowed me to see China through Chinese eyes.

    These moments made me realize how misleading media portrayals can be, and how important it is to witness authenticity firsthand. That desire inspired my first adventure series, “The Girl Without a Map,” where I wandered around Nanjing (and nearby cities) without using maps or setting destinations — letting people, intuition, and chance guide me.

    Later, my second series, “Diaries of Chinese Streets,” began when I overheard a mother’s simple words to her child — trivial, yet they lingered in my mind all day until I wrote them down. It reminded me how everyday moments can hold quiet beauty.

    I’m now working as a social media manager for Western media in Hangzhou, China. Transitioning from student to professional life abroad has been a leap, but one filled with growth and learning.

    My goal is to keep experiencing authenticity and sharing the stories of people who often go unnoticed. I believe every person has a story worth hearing, and every place has something worth sharing. We just have to stop, look, and listen.

    Beijing, September 2024
